Mutual Funds are investment vehicles that pool money from multiple investors to invest in stocks, bonds, or other securities. They are managed by professional fund managers who aim to generate returns based on the fund‚Äôs objective.

What is a Mutual Fund?

A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment fund that collects money from many investors and invests it in a diversified portfolio of assets like equities, debt, or both. Each investor owns units of the fund proportional to their investment.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Mutual Funds

Advantages

  • Professional management
  • Diversification reduces risk
  • Low minimum investment
  • Liquidity (especially open-ended funds)
  • Tax benefits under Section 80C (for ELSS)
  • Transparency and regulation

Disadvantages

  • Expense ratio reduces returns
  • Performance depends on fund manager
  • Market risk applies
  • Exit loads may apply
  • Not suitable for short-term goals

Who Are Fund Managers?

Fund managers are experienced professionals employed by Asset Management Companies (AMCs) to manage mutual fund portfolios. They research, select, and monitor investments to maximize returns while managing risks.

Top AMCs in India: HDFC AMC, SBI Mutual Fund, ICICI Prudential, Axis Mutual Fund, Kotak Mahindra.

Expense Ratio and How It Matters

What is NAV?

Net Asset Value (NAV) is the price per unit of a mutual fund. It is calculated daily by dividing the total value of the fund's assets minus liabilities by the number of outstanding units.

Formula: NAV = (Assets ‚Äì Liabilities) / Total Units Outstanding

üìå NAV does not reflect future returns. A low NAV doesn't mean a better investment.

Regulatory Mechanism

Mutual funds in India are regulated by the **Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI)** and monitored by the **Association of Mutual Funds in India (AMFI)**.

  • SEBI: Sets rules, protects investors, ensures transparency
  • AMFI: Promotes ethical practices, educates investors
  • All AMCs must be registered with SEBI
  • Funds must disclose performance, fees, and risks
